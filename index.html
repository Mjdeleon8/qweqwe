What is Dissection?

Dissecting preserved frogs is a common way to learn about the insides of animals and how their bodies work. It helps us understand how animals adapted over time, like when they moved from living in water to living on land

Importance of Dissection

Dissection allows students to see, touch and explore the various organs. Seeing organs and understanding how they work within a single animal may strengthen studentsâ€™ comprehension of biological systems.

Purpose of the Dissection

Dissecting frogs in school helps students learn about animal bodies by doing it themselves. This hands-on experience makes it easier to understand how animals are built inside.
